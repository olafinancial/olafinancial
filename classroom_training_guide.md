# Pul Planning — Classroom Training & Financial Handbook

This guide serves as a manual for educators running personal finance classes using the Pul Planning web platform. It details classroom setup procedures, student workflows, and a taxonomy of the financial data points tracked.

---

## 1. Online Classroom Setup & Flow

### Step 1: Pre-Class Account Setup
Before the live class, instruct all students to sign up and configure their baseline settings:
1. **Access Pul Planning** via the classroom link.
2. **Sign Up**: Create an account with their email and password.
3. **Reset and Sandbox (Optional)**: If they want to test with synthetic data rather than their own, students can navigate to **Settings → Testing & Account** and click **"Load Demo Data"**. This instantly pre-populates their dashboard with realistic income, expenses, assets, and liabilities.
4. **Base Currency**: Set the preferred classroom currency (e.g., NGN, USD, CAD, or "NONE" to hide prefixes) under **Settings**.

### Step 2: Complete the Guided Onboarding
Students should complete the 6-step Onboarding wizard:
1. **Basic Info**: Name, age, state/residency.
2. **Employment**: Job type, income frequency, and retirement target age.
3. **Financial Goals**: Establish immediate targets (e.g., Emergency Fund, home purchase, savings).
4. **Estate Planning**: Acknowledge basic legal wills and guardian preferences.
5. **Path Checklist**: Preview the standard sequence (Income → Expenses → Net Worth → Goals).
6. **Summary**: Confirm parameters and enter the app shell.

### Step 3: Interactive Live Class Walkthrough
During the online presentation, the instructor can screen-share and guide students through these key pillars:
- **Pillar 1: Income & Deductibles**: Explain gross vs. net pay. Have students look at the Salary Calculator under the **Calculators** hub to model tax brackets (Nigeria Tax Act 2025 brackets or custom rules).
- **Pillar 2: Budgeting**: Demonstrate the **50/30/20 rule** on the **Budget page**. Contrast Needs vs. Wants.
- **Pillar 3: Balance Sheet & Net Worth**: Walk through the separation of Assets (income-generating vs. non-income) and Liabilities (interest-bearing vs. Qard Hasan 0% loans).

## 2. Taxonomy of Tracked Financial Data

Pul Planning organizes and stores user financial data across these primary concepts:

| Category | Specific Data Points | Purpose |
|----------|----------------------|---------|
| **User Profile** | Name, age, retirement age, base currency, Sharia preferences | Personalizes calculators, recommendations, and Zakat calculations |
| **Income Entries** | Source name, type (Active, Passive, Investment), gross/net amount, frequency (biweekly, monthly, semi-annual), deduction breakdowns (pension, NHF) | Drives cash flow inflows, savings rate indicators, and visual income breakdown segments |
| **Expense Entries** | Category, amount, date, frequency, `is_discretionary` flag, amortization intervals (for prepaid items like rent) | Powers the 50/30/20 budget classifier, cash flow outflows, and alerts for overspending |
| **Assets** | Name, type (Cash, Equity, Property, etc.), institutional home, opening balance, interest rate, income-generating toggle, emergency fund source toggle, ticker symbol (for stock market-to-market updates) | Calculates total assets, Emergency Fund coverage metrics, and asset allocations |
| **Liabilities** | Name, type (Credit card, Mortgage, Personal, Qard Hasan), lender, current balance, APR (interest rate), monthly payment schedule | Powers the Debt Planner's Snowball vs. Avalanche strategy timelines and liability ratios |
| **Goals** | Goal name, target amount, deadline date, priority status | Maps monthly surplus allocations (PYF) to visual progress monitors |

## 3. Recommended Classroom Exercise: "The 30-Day Budget Optimization"
1. Have students click **"Reset My Data"** in Settings.
2. Guide them to add 1 Active Income source (Salary) and 3 Expenses (Rent, Groceries, Entertainment).
3. Review the **Budget page**: observe how the Needs/Wants progress rings reflect this.
4. Have them adjust their target percentages to see the visual dashboard change in real-time.
